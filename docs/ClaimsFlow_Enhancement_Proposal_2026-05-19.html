ClaimsFlow
Functional Requirements & Fine-Tuning Proposal

A team-lead and business-analyst view of (i) the new functional capabilities to add to the medical claims platform, and (ii) the targeted fine-tuning of features already in production — ordered by business value, member impact, and delivery effort.
Prepared for: Head of Digital, Claims Operations, CIO Office
Prepared by: ClaimsFlow Engineering & BA
Date: 19 May 2026
Platform release: v1.7 (post maker-checker refactor, scan metering live)
Status: Draft v2 · supersedes v1.0 (19 May 2026)
v2 changes: regulatory theme removed; replaced with explicit functional-requirement statements per card and a new Part II covering fine-tuning of existing features.

1 · Executive summary

ClaimsFlow has matured into a production-grade platform: batch intake with barcoded provider submissions, OCR + AI extraction (Gemini / Ollama / Tesseract), maker-checker dual control, SLA tracking, appeals, payment advice, pre-authorisation, ML fraud scoring with a Python sidecar, line-item extraction, and a labelling deep-analysis tab. The recent rollout of per-provider scan metering closes out a strong v1.x cycle.

This v2 paper proposes the next round of capabilities in two parts:

  • Part I — Functional requirements: new capabilities the system does not yet have. Each item is stated as a numbered functional requirement with clear acceptance criteria.
  • Part II — Fine-tuning & optimisation: targeted improvements to features already in production. These are not new features — they are calibration, tuning, and quality-of-life changes that increase the value of what we have already shipped.

Functional Requirements

2 · Theme A — Member & Provider Experience

Today the policyholder cannot see, submit, or appeal a claim without going through a provider or call-centre. Closing that loop is the single biggest brand and NPS lever available to us.

A1. Member mobile app (React Native) — submit, track, appeal

Value: High Effort: XL Q3 2026 (MVP)
Photo-capture of receipts, real-time claim status, e-card display, dependant management, push notifications, digital pre-auth requests, and direct appeals. Reuses existing consent ledger and JWT auth.
FR-A1.1 A member SHALL be able to register, log in, and complete 2FA from the mobile app using the same credentials as the web portal.
FR-A1.2 A member SHALL be able to submit a new claim by capturing one or more receipt photos, selecting a dependant if applicable, and entering an incident date.
FR-A1.3 The system SHALL display the live status (received / under review / awaiting maker / awaiting checker / paid / rejected / appealed) for every claim attached to the member or their dependants.
FR-A1.4 A member SHALL be able to lodge an appeal against any rejected claim from within the app, attaching supporting documents.
FR-A1.5 The app SHALL display a digital e-card with member number, plan name, and validity that can be shown to providers.

A2. WhatsApp Business API channel — submit + status + 2FA OTP

Value: High Effort: M Q3 2026
WhatsApp is the de-facto messaging channel for members in EA. Offer "send a photo of the receipt" intake, claim-status queries, and OTP delivery alongside the existing SMS provider. The OCR/AI pipeline is already channel-agnostic — we need only the inbound webhook adapter and a session state machine.
FR-A2.1 A member SHALL be able to start a session by sending any message from their registered WhatsApp number and be authenticated via OTP echoed back through the same channel.
FR-A2.2 The system SHALL accept image attachments via WhatsApp, route them through the same OCR pipeline as web uploads, and create a draft claim awaiting member confirmation.
FR-A2.3 A member SHALL be able to query the status of any claim by sending its reference number; the bot SHALL reply with the current state and SLA countdown.
FR-A2.4 The system SHALL persist all WhatsApp interactions against the claim audit log with the same retention rules as web interactions.

A3. Provider Portal v2 — claims search, dispute, statements, scorecard

Value: Medium Effort: M Q4 2026
The provider surface today is "upload batches". Add a self-service view for: claim status by reference, rejection reasons with one-click resubmission, monthly statement and remittance download, and the provider scorecard we already compute internally.
FR-A3.1 A provider_admin SHALL be able to search claims by date range, status, member, or amount, scoped to their own organisation.
FR-A3.2 A provider SHALL be able to view the rejection reason on any rejected claim and submit additional documents in a single action, generating a re-review event.
FR-A3.3 A provider SHALL be able to download monthly remittance statements in PDF and CSV.
FR-A3.4 A provider SHALL be able to view their own scorecard (currently visible only to internal users), with trend over the last 12 months.

A4. In-app feedback & NPS module

Value: Strategic Effort: S Q4 2026
FR-A4.1 The system SHALL prompt a member for an NPS rating (0–10) and an optional free-text comment within 24 hours of a claim reaching a terminal state (paid or rejected).
FR-A4.2 An admin SHALL be able to view an NPS dashboard segmented by claim type, provider, and rejection-reason category.

3 · Theme B — Payments & Financial Integrations

Payment advice is generated, but the rails to actually move money and reconcile back into the GL are still partially manual. This theme attacks the highest-friction hand-off in the business.

B1. M-Pesa B2C / Airtel Money payout rail with auto-reconciliation

Value: High Effort: L Q3 2026
Many small-provider and member-reimbursement payouts are below the EFT economical threshold. Wiring M-Pesa B2C and Airtel Money behind the existing Payment module — with idempotent callback handling and automatic two-way reconciliation against the payment-advice ledger — would compress the advice-to-funds-cleared cycle from days to minutes for the bulk of small payouts.
FR-B1.1 The system SHALL initiate an M-Pesa B2C or Airtel Money payout from an approved payment advice, given a registered mobile-money number on the payee record.
FR-B1.2 The system SHALL handle the mobile-money callback idempotently, marking the payment advice as paid only after a confirmed success callback.
FR-B1.3 The system SHALL retry transient failures up to a configurable limit and surface terminal failures to Finance with the carrier error code.
FR-B1.4 The system SHALL produce a daily mobile-money reconciliation report matching initiated payouts to confirmed transfers.

B2. ERP / GL posting connector (SAP / Oracle / Sage)

Value: High Effort: L Q4 2026
FR-B2.1 The system SHALL emit a daily posting feed of paid claims, accruals, and provider liabilities, mapped to the customer's chart of accounts.
FR-B2.2 The system SHALL maintain a replay-safe posting log such that a re-run on the same day produces no duplicate journal entries.
FR-B2.3 An admin SHALL be able to configure the chart-of-accounts mapping without an engineering release.

B3. Bank statement ingestion + reconciliation engine

Value: Medium Effort: M Q1 2027
FR-B3.1 The system SHALL ingest daily bank statements in MT940, camt.053, or CSV formats.
FR-B3.2 The system SHALL match incoming statement lines to issued payment advices using reference number, amount, and value-date heuristics.
FR-B3.3 The system SHALL present unreconciled items as a Finance queue with manual-match and write-off actions.

B4. Co-pay / deductible / plan-limit calculator engine

Value: Medium Effort: M Q4 2026
FR-B4.1 The system SHALL compute, for any invoice, the payable amount given the member's plan, year-to-date consumption, dependant relationship, and benefit-category limits.
FR-B4.2 The system SHALL expose the calculation breakdown (gross, sub-limit cap, deductible, co-pay, net payable) on the claim detail view and on member-facing surfaces.
FR-B4.3 An admin SHALL be able to model new plan structures without an engineering release.

4 · Theme C — Intelligence & Automation

We have a strong fraud-scoring foundation (GBM sidecar, 12 heuristic signals, self-calibrating weights, human-in-the-loop labels). The next moves shift from structured to unstructured signals — narratives, images, and conversational interaction.

C1. Image-tamper / receipt-forgery detector in the ML sidecar

Value: High Effort: M Q3 2026
FR-C1.1 The ML sidecar SHALL expose a /score-image endpoint that returns a tamper-probability score and a list of triggered indicators (ELA artefacts, EXIF inconsistency, perceptual-hash collision, font-substitution anomaly).
FR-C1.2 Every claim document image SHALL be scored at ingestion and the score persisted with the document record.
FR-C1.3 A configurable threshold SHALL automatically raise a fraud signal when the tamper-probability exceeds the threshold.

C2. Conversational claim assistant — RAG over SRD, policy docs, claim history

Value: High Effort: L Q4 2026
FR-C2.1 A claims officer SHALL be able to ask natural-language questions against an index of the SRD, policy plans, prior appeal decisions, and circulars, and receive a grounded answer with source citations.
FR-C2.2 The assistant SHALL refuse to answer questions outside the indexed corpus rather than confabulating.
FR-C2.3 All assistant interactions SHALL be logged for audit and quality-review purposes.

C3. Auto-triage & straight-through processing for low-risk claims

Value: High Effort: M Q4 2026
Define a "green-lane" policy: claims under value threshold X, from providers with scorecard ≥ Y, OCR confidence ≥ Z, no fraud signals, no plan-limit conflict — auto-approve and route directly to payment. Maker-Checker only on amber/red. Expected to remove 30–50% of queue volume; we have all the inputs already.
FR-C3.1 An admin SHALL be able to define a green-lane policy as a set of rules over claim value, provider scorecard, OCR confidence, fraud score, and plan-limit availability.
FR-C3.2 A claim that satisfies the green-lane policy SHALL bypass the maker and checker queues and proceed directly to payment, with a clear audit marker indicating auto-approval.
FR-C3.3 The system SHALL produce a daily green-lane summary report (count, total value, breakdown by provider).
FR-C3.4 A claims officer SHALL be able to disable the green-lane globally or for a specific provider at any time.

C4. NLP on clinical notes & diagnosis narratives

Value: Medium Effort: L Q1 2027
FR-C4.1 The system SHALL extract ICD-10 and CPT code hints, medication mentions, and red-flag phrasing from free-text clinical notes attached to a claim.
FR-C4.2 The system SHALL surface a discrepancy when extracted clinical hints are inconsistent with the billed procedure codes.

C5. Multilingual support — Swahili extraction + UI

Value: Strategic Effort: M Q1 2027
FR-C5.1 The system SHALL allow each user to select English or Kiswahili as the UI language; all member-facing screens, SMS, WhatsApp, and email content SHALL respect this preference.
FR-C5.2 The OCR extraction prompts SHALL be tuned to handle Kiswahili-language receipts and notes.

5 · Theme D — Clinical & Wellness Extensions

These move CIC from "the company that pays when you're sick" to "the company that helps you stay well". Slower ROI but materially differentiating against payer-only competitors.

D1. HL7 / FHIR connector for hospital HMIS systems

Value: High Effort: L Q4 2026
Top-tier hospitals already run HMIS platforms emitting HL7 v2 or FHIR R4. A direct connector eliminates the batch-scan step for those providers — pre-auth requests, discharge summaries, and itemised bills arrive structured. Removes OCR cost and error rate for our highest-volume providers.
FR-D1.1 The system SHALL accept inbound HL7 v2 ADT, DFT, and ORM messages, and FHIR R4 Claim, Patient, and Encounter resources.
FR-D1.2 Inbound HMIS-sourced claims SHALL be flagged as structured-source and bypass the OCR pipeline entirely.
FR-D1.3 The system SHALL emit FHIR ClaimResponse resources back to the originating HMIS once a decision is reached.

D2. Telemedicine session booking + claim auto-creation

Value: Medium Effort: L Q1 2027
FR-D2.1 A member SHALL be able to book a tele-consultation slot from the mobile app, subject to outpatient cover availability.
FR-D2.2 The completed consultation SHALL produce a structured claim automatically, with the consultation note attached.

D3. Pharmacy Benefit Manager (PBM) module

Value: Medium Effort: L Q2 2027
FR-D3.1 The system SHALL perform a real-time eligibility + formulary check given a member ID and a list of prescribed drugs.
FR-D3.2 The system SHALL return drug-drug interaction warnings and generic-substitution prompts as part of the eligibility response.

D4. Chronic-disease management cohort tracking

Value: Strategic Effort: M Q2 2027
FR-D4.1 The system SHALL identify members with qualifying chronic conditions based on claim history.
FR-D4.2 The system SHALL surface care-gap alerts (e.g. missed follow-up, missed annual screening) to the member and to Claims Operations.

6 · Theme E — Platform Architecture & SRE

The platform runs well today but it is single-tenant, modestly observable, and the DR story is "we have backups". This theme prepares it to operate at insurer-scale and, eventually, to be white-labelled.

E1. Observability uplift — OpenTelemetry, SLO dashboards, error budgets

Value: Medium Effort: M Q3 2026
FR-E1.1 The backend SHALL emit OpenTelemetry traces for every HTTP request, OCR call, and ML-sidecar call.
FR-E1.2 The system SHALL publish a named set of SLOs (claim-submit p95, OCR p95, fraud-score p95, end-to-end claim cycle time) on a public-internal dashboard with error-budget burn alerts.

E2. Disaster recovery automation + quarterly tabletop

Value: Medium Effort: M Q4 2026
FR-E2.1 The system SHALL run an automated daily restore drill against a staging cluster from the latest production backup.
FR-E2.2 The system SHALL publish RTO and RPO measurements against documented targets on a monthly basis.

E3. SSO via Azure AD / Okta (OIDC + SAML)

Value: Medium Effort: M Q4 2026
FR-E3.1 Internal users SHALL be able to authenticate via the corporate identity provider using OIDC or SAML.
FR-E3.2 Leaver de-provisioning at the identity provider SHALL revoke ClaimsFlow access within 15 minutes.

E4. Feature-flag service + canary deploys

Value: Low Effort: S Q4 2026
FR-E4.1 The system SHALL support feature flags with targeting by role, provider, and percentage rollout.
FR-E4.2 An admin SHALL be able to toggle a flag without redeploying.

E5. Multi-tenancy (white-label for partner insurers / brokers)

Value: Strategic Effort: XL Q2 2027
FR-E5.1 The system SHALL isolate data, branding, and configuration per tenant such that no cross-tenant data leakage is possible.
FR-E5.2 An admin SHALL be able to provision a new tenant without code changes.

7 · Theme F — Workflow & Case Management

The current pipeline is excellent for the 80% case but rigid for the 20% — complex claims, multi-document appeals, internal escalations. This theme makes the system handle exceptions as a first-class shape.

F1. Case-management surface for complex / escalated claims

Value: Medium Effort: M Q4 2026
FR-F1.1 A claims officer SHALL be able to convert any claim into a "case" that aggregates the claim, related documents, appeals, fraud verdicts, internal notes, and correspondence.
FR-F1.2 A case SHALL support assignment to a named owner, internal comments, and a chronological timeline view.
FR-F1.3 A case SHALL have its own SLA, configurable separately from the underlying claim SLA.

F2. Correspondence / letter-generation module

Value: Medium Effort: S Q4 2026
FR-F2.1 An admin SHALL be able to define templated letters (rejection, appeal outcome, member acknowledgement) with merge fields.
FR-F2.2 A claims officer SHALL be able to generate a letter from a template against a claim or case in one action, producing a PDF that is emailed and filed against the audit trail.

F3. Bulk operations & supervisor-grade actions

Value: Low Effort: S Q1 2027
FR-F3.1 A claims officer SHALL be able to multi-select items in any queue view and apply approve, reassign, or export actions in a single operation.
FR-F3.2 Bulk operations SHALL be limited to the claims_officer role and SHALL preserve maker-checker separation on individual approvals.

F4. Visual workflow designer (low-code, BPMN-lite)

Value: Strategic Effort: L Q2 2027
FR-F4.1 An admin SHALL be able to add, remove, and reorder workflow steps, set SLA timers, and define branching rules without an engineering release.
FR-F4.2 The workflow engine SHALL version each published workflow and allow rollback to a prior version.
Part II
Fine-Tuning & Optimisation
Calibration, tuning, and quality-of-life improvements to features already in production. No new infrastructure — just better outcomes from what we have already shipped.

8 · Track T1 — OCR & Extraction Pipeline Tuning

The current pipeline runs Gemini Vision → Ollama → Tesseract as a fallback chain. The tuning gains here are pure prompt and threshold work, no new infrastructure.

T1.1 Gemini extraction prompt refinement & structured output schemas

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
Rework the Gemini extraction prompt to request a strictly-typed JSON schema response (invoice header, line items, totals, tax) and enforce schema validation at the boundary. Expected: a measurable reduction in line-item misalignment errors and a sharper signal on which fields the model is uncertain about. Target: reduce manual line-item corrections per claim from current baseline by 25–35%.

T1.2 Fallback-chain decision matrix (Gemini → Ollama → Tesseract)

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
Today the chain falls through on hard failure. Tune it to fall through on confidence — when Gemini returns a result but the field-level confidence is below a threshold, run Ollama as a second opinion and arbitrate by agreement. Expected: better extraction on poor-quality scans without raising cost on clean scans. Target: raise first-pass extraction acceptance rate on the bottom-quartile scan-quality bucket by 10–15 percentage points.

T1.3 Image preprocessing — deskew, denoise, contrast normalisation

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
Insert an OpenCV preprocessing step before OCR — deskew, denoise, adaptive thresholding, perspective correction. Cheap on CPU; meaningful uplift on phone-camera receipts where the source quality is the bottleneck. The ML sidecar already does image-quality scoring, so we have the inputs to know when preprocessing should be applied.

T1.4 Line-item extraction confidence-gated auto-flow

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
Gate per-line-item confidence at the row level rather than the document level. High-confidence rows flow through; low-confidence rows are highlighted in the maker UI for targeted review rather than forcing a re-key of the entire invoice.

T1.5 Document-classifier taxonomy expansion + confusion-matrix-driven retraining

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
The "Unknown Documents" queue is itself a labelling source. Use last quarter's confusion matrix to identify the document classes that are most often misclassified, expand the taxonomy where genuinely new types are emerging, and retrain. Expected: visible reduction in the size of the Unknown Documents queue without changing the classifier architecture.

9 · Track T2 — Fraud-Scoring Model Tuning

The fraud sidecar is healthy and the human-in-the-loop labelling loop works. The tuning here is about turning that label stream into ongoing accuracy improvements without engineering intervention each cycle.

T2.1 Automated weekly retrain from labelled data + drift detection

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
Schedule a weekly retrain job that pulls the latest labelled claims, refits the gradient-boosting classifier, evaluates it on a held-out set, and promotes the new model only if AUC and precision-at-K are non-degraded. Drift detection on feature distributions raises an alert when the input shape moves away from the training distribution. Target: reduce model-staleness lag from "whenever someone remembers" to ≤ 7 days.

T2.2 Per-provider threshold calibration

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
A single global fraud-score threshold is the wrong shape: high-volume reputable providers tolerate a higher threshold, low-volume new providers a lower one. Calibrate per-provider thresholds against historical false-positive and false-negative rates, recomputed monthly. Target: measurable reduction in false-positive fraud flags on top-quartile providers, while holding catch rate constant overall.

T2.3 Heuristic-signal weight rebalancing from labelled outcomes

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
The 12 heuristic detectors have self-calibrating weights already. Add a quarterly review surface that shows analysts which signals are pulling their weight (true-positive contribution) and which are noise, so the signal set itself can be pruned or refined over time.

T2.4 Cross-provider duplicate-detection window tuning

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
The cross-provider duplicate check looks back over a fixed window. Tune the window per claim type: pharmacy duplicates often surface within days; inpatient duplicates across weeks. A single window misses both ends.

10 · Track T3 — Workflow, SLA & Queue Routing Tuning

The maker-checker workflow is correct; the tuning is in how work is distributed and how SLAs are measured.

T3.1 SLA timer per claim-value band + business-hours awareness

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
A flat SLA penalises low-value claims and under-pressures high-value ones. Define SLA bands by claim value and exclude weekends / public holidays from the elapsed-time count. Aging dashboard becomes a truer signal.

T3.2 Maker-checker assignment — load-balancing + anti-collusion + complexity-aware

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
Three small changes to the routing rule:
  1. Load-balance across currently-active staff (skip leave/inactive users).
  2. Anti-collusion: the maker and checker for a given provider's claim must not be the same individual.
  3. Complexity-aware: route claims with above-median OCR-correction or fraud-score to more experienced staff (configurable per-user seniority tier).

T3.3 Notification batching, digest mode, and quiet-hours

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
The notifications module currently fires per event. Add per-user digest mode (hourly / daily) and quiet-hours that hold non-urgent notifications until the next active window. Reduces notification fatigue without dropping urgent alerts (SLA breach, fraud confirmed) which always go through.

T3.4 Provider scorecard formula rebalance — quality vs. volume dimensions

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
The current scorecard blends volume and quality into a single number. Split it into two: a quality score (rejection rate, fraud rate, OCR re-key rate) and a volume score (claims per month, member reach). Together they support better-shaped decisions — e.g. green-laning a high-quality, low-volume provider that the blended score under-rates today.

T3.5 Scan-metering thresholds & budget pacing alerts

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
Scan metering is now live. Tune the per-tier thresholds against the first 30 days of real consumption data, and add proactive pacing alerts ("provider X is on track to exceed monthly quota by day 22") rather than only after-the-fact breach notifications.

11 · Track T4 — Performance & Data-Layer Tuning

No re-platform, no new infrastructure — just attacking the known hot-spots in the current Postgres / Prisma / Redis stack.

T4.1 Eliminate N+1 query patterns on the workflow dashboard

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
Audit the Prisma include/select trees on the workflow, aging, and maker/checker queue endpoints; batch or join where queries are firing per row. Largest single dashboard-latency lever available without new infrastructure.

T4.2 Targeted Postgres indexes on hot query paths

Tuning Value: High Effort: S
Use pg_stat_statements to find the top-20 slowest recurring queries and add the missing composite indexes (status + assigned-to + created-at on the workflow queues is a known one). A migration-only change, but typically the highest ROI tuning in any maturing Postgres-backed system.

T4.3 Postgres trigram indexes for free-text claim search

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
Add pg_trgm GIN indexes on claim reference, member name, and provider name to make the global search bar perceptibly faster on large tables. No new tech, just an extension and a migration.

T4.4 Redis-backed memoisation for plan-rules evaluation

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
Plan-rules evaluation is deterministic for a given member + date + plan version. Memoise the result in Redis with a short TTL; invalidate on plan-version change. Removes a measurable share of the eligibility endpoint's tail latency.

T4.5 Activity-log partitioning by month

Tuning Value: Low Effort: S
The activity_logs table grows monotonically. Range-partition by month; query patterns are typically date-bounded, so query time stays flat as history accumulates. Drop-old-partition becomes a one-line archival operation.

T4.6 Frontend bundle splitting per route group

Tuning Value: Medium Effort: S
Code-split the frontend along role boundaries — admin, finance, provider, claims-officer — so each role only downloads the bundle they actually need. Vite supports this natively; the win is on cold-start time, especially for provider users on mobile data.

T4.7 WebSocket heartbeat + reconnect-backoff tuning

Tuning Value: Low Effort: S
Real-time notifications run over Socket.IO. Tune heartbeat interval and reconnect backoff so flaky connections degrade gracefully instead of disconnecting and missing notifications.

13 · What I am explicitly not recommending (yet)

  • A full custom LLM trained on CIC claims data. The RAG approach in C2 captures most of the value at a fraction of the cost and risk. Revisit only if RAG performance plateaus.
  • Replacing PostgreSQL with a "claims-specific" database. Schema and Prisma are healthy. Performance gaps should be closed with the T4 tuning, not a re-platform.
  • Native mobile app for staff. Claims officers, makers, and checkers work from desks; the existing web app is sufficient. The mobile app investment goes to members and providers.
  • Rewriting the fraud sidecar in a different ML framework. The T2 tuning track shows the existing scikit-learn pipeline still has substantial headroom from data and threshold work alone.
  • Workflow designer (F4) before we know the workflow needs to change. Park until we have evidence operations want to reshape the pipeline often enough to justify the abstraction.
